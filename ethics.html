Responsible use in the academe

An excerpt from British School Manila. Learn more.

AI can be an incredibly powerful tool, but it’s essential that students develop critical thinking skills. Students should learn not only how to use AI tools but also how to question and assess the information provided by AI systems.

Encouraging students to approach AI with curiosity, skepticism, and open-mindedness will help them make informed decisions about how and when to rely on AI in their lives.

An excerpt from University Canada West. Learn more.

The impact of AI in education has been transformative and far-reaching. AI technologies enhance learning experiences by enabling personalized education (apps like NotebookLM), automating administrative tasks and offering intelligent tutoring systems that adapt to each student’s pace and ability.

However, there is also a negative impact of artificial intelligence on students that must be considered. Overreliance on AI tools can lead to reduced critical thinking and problem-solving skills, as students may depend too heavily on automated assistance.

AI and its tools should support learning, not replace it.
Whenever you need a litte bit of advice, AI can suffice. But you still need to discern and double-check what the AI outputs back.
In the end, it's all about using the right tools in the right way.

An excerpt from United Nations University. Some paragraphs are paraphrased. Click here to see the full article.

AI can gives predictions with a given, existing set of data or expected outcomes.

Although predictions can be entirely correct within the scope of its data. It can also be false due to external factors it cannot predict or account for.

For instance, an AI model predicting stock market trends with high accuracy might correctly forecast price movements based on historical data patterns and real-time market analysis. Yet, this accuracy does not mean it can predict the next rise or fall of a market value every time.

This is mostly straightforward, as you just need to apply critical thinking when trying to verify if what the AI says it true or false.

Here's an excerpt from Public Relations Society of America. Although the article is slightly out of topic in relation to academe, it is still a worthwhile read. Click here to see the full article.

Look at the source material.
Check the source material to see if an author and/or a credible publication is listed. Be sure to look at the date of the original material to make sure it’s recent and/or relevant.

Trust your gut.
View content in a way that an AI bot never will. When you’re reading through the data or writing produced by a Generative AI platform, trust any uncertainty or uneasiness you feel, and do your due diligence to check the work.

Students have to constantly think creatively with new perspectives. Generative AI can help us do that by making us work more efficiently, but taking the time to fact-check and ensure its accuracy is an essential step.
